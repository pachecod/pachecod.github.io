An interactive exploration of American military presence across global regions over the past seven decades. Navigate through different regions to discover deployment patterns and historical trends.

Overview

This visualization presents a comprehensive analysis of U.S. military troop deployments across different regions of the world from 1950 to the present. The data reflects significant geopolitical shifts, military interventions, and strategic priorities throughout the Cold War, post-Cold War era, and contemporary period.

Antarctica

Antarctica maintains a unique status in international relations, governed by the Antarctic Treaty System which dedicates the continent to peaceful purposes and scientific research. U.S. military presence in this region is minimal and primarily focused on logistical support for scientific missions.

Sub-Saharan Africa

U.S. military presence in Sub-Saharan Africa has evolved from minimal Cold War engagement to more substantial involvement in counterterrorism, peacekeeping, and capacity-building missions in the 21st century.

The establishment of U.S. Africa Command (AFRICOM) in 2008 marked a significant shift in U.S. military engagement with the continent, coordinating operations across 53 African countries.
